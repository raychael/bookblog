Title: Crying in H Mart

Author: Michelle Zauner

Rating:

Thoughts: Crying in H Mart is a powerful memoir about a daughter's experience losing her mother and their rocky relationship throughout her life.

Firstly, I want to say I really admire Zauner's bravery publishing such a personal memoir. I know it couldn't have been easy and despite some of my feelings while reading the book it's still very admirable.

I struggled with a couple of things reading this memoir. The first one being, I've never had someone extremely close, like a mother, die in my life. I kept thinking throughout my reading experience that I would have been getting a lot more out of it had I related on a deeper level. Another issue I had was the relationship that Michelle had with her mother as she grew older wasn't exactly relatable to me. There are a lot of moments in the book where Zauner refers to her mother and her being the same person. As much as a love and cherish my own mother, I can't relate to that.

Lastly, this book felt a lot more like a private diary entry than a published book. It gets pretty repetitive towards the end because of the authors venting. Which is understantable but not something you want in a published work. Oveall though, I would say this is worth the read. Especially if you are someone who has lost a loved one recently and you need a book to help you grieve.
