Title: The Whisper Man

Author: Alex North

Rating:

Thoughts: Thoughts: I feel a similar way about the Whisper Man that I do with most of the “scary” books I’ve been reading. What I mean is that I really enjoyed this book, I thought the overall plot was fantastic and loved the boogie monster aspect to the kidnapper. However, I wanted to be genuinely scared from this book, and I really wasn’t, which brought it down to a 4 star.

Another thing that I found hard to deal with was Jake’s character, the little kid we follow closely throughout the book. I get it, he’s a little kid and we can’t expect him to act maturely or rationally. But he was, for lack of a better word, annoying.

However, there was a lot of good in this book. There’s mystery, romance, loss and grief, and a really challenging father and son relationship dynamic that our characters have to learn to navigate.

I say read this. I’ll definitely be reading more by this author in the future.
