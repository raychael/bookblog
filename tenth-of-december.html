Title: Tenth of December

Author: George Saunders

Rating:

Thoughts: George Saunders is an author I was first introduced to my freshman year of college. And ever since then I haven’t been able to get his writing out of my head. It’s by far the most unique style of writing I’ve ever read. So unique, in fact, you just have to read it to know what I’m talking about.

I’m sure his writing style doesn’t work for everyone, but for me it was a game changer. I’ve been wanting to read Tenth of December (a short story collection) for a long time now and I’m so glad I picked it up.

The reason this book wasn’t an immediate five star read for me was because of the fact it’s a short story collection and I just didn’t love all of the stories. I liked a lot of them though, which is why I think this book is very much worth reading.

George Saunders addresses real issues and thought provoking questions in his stories while also being very out there in terms of the story lines. There was one short story in particular, The Semplica Girl Diaries, that I still can’t stop thinking about. Truly haunting.
