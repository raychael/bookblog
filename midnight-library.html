Title: The Midnight Library

Author: Matt Haig

Rating:

Thoughts: The Midnight Library is a fun, quirky, and ridiculously cheesy fiction book about why you should decide to live. It’s a nice message, in theory, but in execution it didn’t work for me. I think it’s a difficult message to give without sounding corny and a little ignorant to mental illness.

Despite the cheesiness I actually had a lot of fun reading this book. I liked our main character, Nora, and seeing the different lives she led. It’s a light read, 304 pages with large text, and the adventures our main character goes on are (for the most part) entertaining.

I say for the most part because there are only so many adventures a book can take me on before it feels like it’s becoming repetitive, which it was. Nora ended up being a lot of different people in her lives and what was meant to be an inspiring message about being exactly where you’re supposed to be ended up being downright depressing. Especially since she didn’t have much going for her in her real life.

My favorite life was Nora’s last one and if I could have had it my way much more of the book would have been centered around that life. It was the closest we got to who Nora really was and we were able to see the blossoming of real connections form. Overall, I say read it, but not if you are looking for a book to give you a reason to live. If that’s the case skip it, and pick up something else. I don’t think this book will help.
