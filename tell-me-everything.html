Title: Tell Me Everything

Author: Erika Krouse

Rating:

Thoughts: A fun fact, this was actually my first 5 star read of 2022. I wasn’t having very much luck with 5 star reads given it’s now March and I think it’s so crazy my first 5 star read is a random book I picked up from Book of the Month that I originally knew nothing about and went into without reading a single review.

Tell Me Everything is fascinating, devastating, tragic, infuriating and pretty much every other dramatic word you can think of. It’s hard to read (lots of trigger warnings with this one so look them up before reading please) and will have you feeling a lot of sadness and closeness to our main characters.

Erika is a private investigator assisting in taking down a university for their negligence with sexual assault and harassment at their university, specifically on their football team and during recruitment.

We hear a lot about Erika’s childhood where she dealt with her own abuse and the impact that is still having on her now, especially given the case she is working.

I loved this book. I think a lot of people could benefit from reading it and if you can handle the trigger warnings, I think you will too.
