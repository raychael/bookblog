Title: The Fireman

Author: Joe Hill

Rating:

Thoughts: The Fireman was a book that while entertaining didn’t make a lot of sense to me. Pandemic story? Interesting. A decease that causes people to light on fire? Okay, less realistic but still cool. A welcoming community that turns out to have cult vibes? Love that. And then people just lighting up like a lightbulb for some reason? Now you’re losing me.

Like I said before I was pretty entertained with this book. Yes, things were pretty unbelievable and strange but a lot of books are so I don’t think that’s a reason to dislike a book per se. But for a book that’s over 700 pages not much seemed to be going on, so that’s why when things did happen I wasn’t too keen on how strange and unbelievable it was.

I also found myself becoming bored with so many pages of the same book to read. I think it takes a lot for an author to keep a reader engaged that long and I simply wasn’t. I do think Joe Hill is a talented writer. I just wish he wouldn’t follow so closely in his dads footsteps with the outrageously long books.
