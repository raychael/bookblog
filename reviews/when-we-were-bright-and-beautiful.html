Title: When We Were Bright and Beautiful

Author: Jillian Medoff

Rating:

Thoughts: This was actually one of those books that upon purchasing I had super high hopes for. That might have been some of the problem with my lack of enjoyment here. When We Were Bright and Beautiful is about a families journey through one of the sons being accused of sexual assault, and how each family member handles the news. Upon hearing this a thought this would be a great book. We have a group of rich and spoliled individuals who are going to be faced with a reality check and a moment to reflect on how they ended up where they did.

The book goes in a very different path, that quite frankly I wasn't a fan of. It still felt to me at the end of the novel our characters never got the growth that they needed and instead stayed stuck in their entitled and out of touch with reality mentality. It was hard and frustrating to read at times because all I wanted was for our characters to wake up and realize what was happending. But they just didn't.

Instead of reading this I would spend your time elsewhere. Maybe pick up Michelle Miller's book "Know My Name." I think you'll find a lot more valuable content there.
