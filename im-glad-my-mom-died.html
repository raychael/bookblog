Title: I'm Glad My Mom Died

Author: Jennette McCurdy

Rating:

Thoughts: Jennette McCurdy's memoir "I'm Glad My Mom Died" is devastating, infuriating, emotional, education, and a lot of other empotions I can't begin to put together because it was just that amazing. This memoir is about abuse, the issues with child acting, fame, and pressure. It's about McCurdy living a life that is not her own to please a narcissist mother who knows no boundaries and the damages that does to McCurdy after her death.

I highly recommend this read if you are prepared for it's contents. It's very well writen, which was something I was nervous about going into it. It's also feels incredibly honestly and raw. It's like you are reading McCurdy's diary for 300 pages in the best way possible.

Honestly, I get why this is so popular. This book deserves that.
