Title: The Girl With the Louding Voice

Author: Abi Daré

Rating:

Thoughts: The girl with the louding voice is a compelling story about a Nigerian girl named Adunni and her quest to get an education and find her own voice.

I felt a lot of different things about this book as I was reading it and I hate to say the first thought was wondering when Adunni’s English would get better so the book became easier to read.

The author did a very interesting thing with the narrative of this book that really sucked the reader in and reminded them constantly they were seeing everything through Adunni’s eyes, as she is the narrator, by writing the entire book in broken English. And while I like the concept I struggled with this. Unlike my previous assumption that Adunni would go to school in the book and her English would improve for us readers over time, that was not the case. The book actually doesn’t follow Adunni in school, or throughout much of her life, like I originally thought.

I probably shouldn’t be disappointed in a book for not being something I assumed it would be, but I couldn’t help it. I wanted to see Adunni live out her dream, I wanted to follow her into adulthood and see how amazing her life turned out, instead of only seeing Adunni in her adolescence.

Despite my wrong assumptions I still really liked this book. I especially liked the educational notes the author left throughout the book.

It was informative, heartbreaking, and difficult to read at times. But I highly recommend it.
