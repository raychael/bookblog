Title: The Song of Achilles

Author: Madeline Miller

Rating:

Thoughts: I’m quite sad because I feel like I may be the only person on the planet that didn’t totally fall in love with this book. I understand why so many people did, but it wasn’t for me.

The best part of this book was just getting to read about greek mythology and how beautiful Madeline Miller describes the scenes. I found myself very impressed with her writing style in this one and wanting to continue just for that purpose. I also don’t know a lot greek mythology, but I really want to learn more, so everything to do with the gods was fascinating and kept me curious.

However, I found myself becoming bored with the politics in this book, and honestly, Achilles and Patroclus’ relationship. There were a lot of meetings in this book, which I understand were necessary but that still didn’t mean I wanted to read about it. I found myself tempted a lot to pick up other books (and did several times) but I still wanted to push through to read about the tragic ending.

No tears were shed, so clearly I didn’t find the ending that heartbreaking. I wasn’t invested enough in the connection between Achilles and Patroclus, so it was hard for me to feel too sad.
